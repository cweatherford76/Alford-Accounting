Meet Joanne Alford

Accountant, financial coach, and proof that debt-free is possible — even when life hands you the hard version.

The honest truth

"Make no mistake — it wasn't easy and I was exhausted. But it was worth every step."

My story

When my son was fifteen months old, I was stuck in a six-hundred-square-foot apartment, facing a pending job loss and thousands of dollars in divorce legal fees. I felt hopeless, stuck, and afraid of the future I could give the two of us.

So I did the only thing I could control: I built a budget, decided to live on less than I made, and went after my debt like it was the plague.

A decade later, I've doubled my income, paid off every penny of consumer debt, and I don't owe anyone anything — no mortgage included. I'm debt free, and I built it the same slow, stubborn way I'll help you build yours.

Life still throws curveballs. The very day I made the final payment on my car loan, the rear tire exploded on my commute home and went flying into a cornfield. If that's not the universe testing me, I don't know what is. I kept going anyway — and that's the point.
